Mehu

Mehu is a mobile app focusing on making the transition of waiting in line to getting a haircut faster, easier and convenient. The founder has a vision where clients could get in line for a haircut electronically and be free to do other stuff while they were waiting in line. It is also a platform where up and coming stylists and artists (hair, makeup, tattoo etc) working out of their homes could have access to new clients.

Before I joined the project, the mobile app had all the aforementioned capabilities however, there were not as many stylists using the platform as expected and clients had complained about the app being inefficient and hard to use.

My role in this project was not only to design the screens for the mobile app but also to facilitate conversations between the Business and Development sides of the project. In addition, I helped shape some of the Business goals for the new release.

Microsoft GDS

While interning at Microsoft GDS in Hyderabad, I was tasked in making designs for a C2C application which had more than 10 nested levels of information that needed to be displayed in a tabular form in a web application and a mobile app. The current designs at that point were not able to display the levels and the hierarchy in a user-friendly fashion and the clients were not happy with the many different designs the team had already presented.

My primary role here was to be a designer and come up with UI that was able to capture the nested level hierarchy as well as show the tabular information associated with selected level in the tree. I researched many different ways and settled on a multi-level menu or a treeview.

A live demo was required for this project, so after I designed the web application and mobile screens, another developer and I wrote the code for these designs using HTML5/CSS3, jQuery, JavaScript ES5 and a plug-in called DLMenu. The code was handed over to the clients and our manager at that time relayed the client's very positive feedback to us.
